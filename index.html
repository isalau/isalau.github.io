Isabel Laurenceau

Welcome! My name is Isabel Laurenceau, I am twenty-five and originally from Miami, Florida. Currently I live in Gainesville, Florida where I am a third year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Florida. I work in the Human Experience Research Lab with my advisor Dr.Juan Gilbert. I received my Master's of Computer Engineering from the Univeristy of Florida in 2019. In 2017 I received my Bachelor of Science degree also from the University of Florida in Computer Engineering with minors in math and dance. My research focuses on affective wearable devices and tech policy.

Experience

For the past three years, Isabel has primarily been a graduate student at the University of Florida. She has spent her summers as a Media Democracy Fund Technology Policy Fellow. Through this, Isabel has gained experience working at two extraordinary non-profits in D.C. Most recently, she worked at The Leadership Conference on Civil and Human Rights with their 2020 Census team. There she worked to create and disseminate digital literacy information for their Get Out The Count ToolKit and around mis/disinformation. She also worked closely with the team and partners on discussion on differential privacy to be used in the 2020 Census. In 2018 she was at Public Knowledge working on Net Neutrality issues. Isabel wrote internal documents about HIPAA and open source licenses. She also wrote a blog post about the effects on the proposed European copyright directive on game creators found here. She aided other interns in research for their blog posts.

Throughout her time as an undergraduate, Isabel worked on campus at the Academic Technology Labs where she served as Supervisor for her last two years with the company. She had two internships as an undergraduate. The final was as an iOS developer with State Farm in Atlanta, Georgia, and the first was with Southern Wine & Spirits in their IT department.
